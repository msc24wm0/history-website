Causes of the Renaissance

  • Decline of Church Authority: Corruption like indulgences sparked reflection; Black Death challenged faith authority
  • Educational Expansion: More universities, richer libraries promoted questioning & knowledge spread
  • Crusades: East-West exchange brought back art & knowledge
  • Trade Growth: Wealthy merchants patronized arts & scholarship

  • Spirit of Inquiry: Challenging authority & old systems (Church doctrine examined)
  • Classical Revival: Architecture, sculpture, painting absorbed Greek & Roman elements; human proportions, perspective, light & shadow

Timeline

Ancient Civilizations

Greek and Roman classical culture laid the foundation, emphasizing humanities, arts and philosophical thinking.

Medieval Period (5th-14th centuries)

Religion dominated society, classical culture declined, but universities and libraries gradually developed.

14th Century: Renaissance Emergence

Italian city-states began reviving classical culture, humanistic thought emerged.

15th-16th Centuries: European Spread

Renaissance thought and art spread to Northern Europe, Scientific Revolution developed simultaneously.

1543: Scientific Revolution Marker

Copernicus published "On the Revolutions of Celestial Spheres," marking the official beginning of the Scientific Revolution.

Modern Period

Rationalism, scientific methods and humanistic spirit became the cornerstone of modern civilization.

Glossary

Humanism

A human-centered philosophical system emphasizing reason, dignity and human potential.

Indulgences

"Sin remission" certificates sold by the Church to raise funds, causing moral controversy.

Perspective

Painting technique creating spatial depth, making images more three-dimensional and realistic.

Chiaroscuro

Artistic technique using light and shadow contrast to create dramatic effects and dimensionality.

Patronage

Economic support system where wealthy merchants and nobles sponsored artists and scholars.

City-states

Independent Italian city nations, such as Florence, Venice, etc.
